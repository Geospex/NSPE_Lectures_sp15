Four Types of Resolution when discussing satellite imagery & Remote Sensing:

 

>Spatial

>Spectral

>Temporal

>Radiometric

Spatial Resolution

pixel size of an image representing the size of the surface area, i.e. the ground, determined by the sensor’s instantaneous field of view (IFOV)

Spectral Resolution:

wavelength interval size (‘segment’ of the electromagnetic spectrum) & # of intervals that the sensor is able to measure

Temporal Resolution:

Amount of time that passes between imagery collection periods for a given surface location

Radiometric Resolution:

Brightness that sensor can record. Bit-depth is the unit by which radiometric resolution is measured:

>8-bit=0-255

>11-bit=0-2047

>12-bit=0-4095

>16-bit=0-65,535
