Map Types & Thematic Mapping

Thematic Mapping:

 

 

A thematic map is a type of map especially designed to show a particular theme connected with a specific geographic area.

 

These maps "can portray physical, social, political, cultural, economic, sociological, agricultural, or any other aspects of a city, state, region, nation, or continent".

Thematic Mapping:

 

 

A thematic map is often used to denote densities, concentrations, populations, ranks, distributions, relative magnitudes and gradients across geographies.

One common, powerful type of thematic map is the Choropleth Map

 

 

....its main purpose is denoting a 'theme across geography' or in the words of the ancient Greeks χώρο ("area/region") + πλήθος ("multitude")
